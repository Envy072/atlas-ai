# Atlas AI — Product Backlog (Post-Phase 1)

**Status:** Permanent product backlog, created from real user testing after
Phase 1.

**This is NOT part of the current milestone implementation.** It does not
modify any code, architecture, or the current milestone roadmap. It exists
only as a product roadmap to execute **after** the current milestone
roadmap (Research → Competitor → Market → Financial → Business → Decision
Intelligence) is complete.

**Phase 1 Overall Product Score: 5/10**

---

## 1. Purpose

This document captures the findings of the first manual product evaluation
of Atlas AI, performed after Phase 1's architecture was in place. Phase 1
built a deep, layered knowledge/decision-intelligence backend; this
backlog captures what a real evaluator experienced when using the product
end to end, and what needs to change before that architecture translates
into something a founder would actually pay for.

**General observation from testing:** the current system feels like a
*knowledge engine* rather than a *product*. It provides general
information but not enough trusted, actionable guidance that would
convince someone to pay for it. Every item below is a direct response to
that gap — either closing the trust gap (evidence, sourcing, confidence),
the experience gap (visibility into what the system is doing), or the
completeness gap (the product stops at analysis instead of carrying a
founder toward a launched startup).

This backlog does not invent new feature ideas beyond what testing
surfaced. It organizes real feedback into priority tiers so it can be
picked up as its own roadmap once the current milestone sequence
finishes.

---

## 2. Product Vision

> Atlas AI should help a founder move from an idea to launching a
> startup — not only produce an AI-generated report.

Phase 1 proved the knowledge architecture (research, competitors, market,
financial, business, and decision synthesis) can be built cleanly and
consistently. The next phase of the product needs to prove that
architecture is trustworthy (real sources, real evidence, real
confidence — not generic text) and that it's useful all the way through
execution, not just at the analysis step. The desired end-to-end
experience, as identified in testing:

```
Idea
  ↓
Research
  ↓
Analysis
  ↓
Decision
  ↓
Execution Plan
  ↓
Weekly Tasks
  ↓
Validation
  ↓
MVP
  ↓
Launch
```

Atlas AI should become an **AI Startup Builder**, not only an **AI
Startup Analyzer**.

---

## 3. Priority 1 — Analysis Experience

- Cannot see the current analysis stage.
- Cannot see how much analysis remains.
- No progress timeline.
- Cannot cancel analysis.
- Cannot restart a single stage.
- No clear execution flow.

---

## 4. Priority 1 — Trust & Evidence

- Most information feels generic.
- Sources are not visible.
- Facts are not clearly referenced.
- Confidence is difficult to trust.
- Conclusions are not sufficiently explained.
- It is unclear which information is verified versus inferred.

---

## 5. Priority 1 — Competitor Intelligence

- Need much deeper competitor profiles.
- Pricing comparison.
- Features comparison.
- Website links.
- Customer complaints.
- Customer reviews.
- Why customers choose them.
- Why customers leave.
- Market positioning.
- Strengths and weaknesses with evidence.

---

## 6. Priority 1 — Market Intelligence

- Current market section is mostly text.
- Need TAM/SAM/SOM with sources.
- Growth charts.
- Geographic breakdown.
- Entry barriers.
- Regulations.
- Search demand.
- Market trends supported by evidence.

---

## 7. Priority 1 — Financial Intelligence

- Financial section is too short.
- Need real calculations.
- Revenue assumptions.
- CAC/LTV explanation.
- Break-even analysis.
- Sensitivity analysis.
- Sources for every financial estimate.

---
